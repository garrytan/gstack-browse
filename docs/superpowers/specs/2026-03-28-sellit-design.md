# Design: /sellit — First 10 Sales Skill

Generated by /brainstorming on 2026-03-28
Branch: main
Repo: gstack
Status: APPROVED

## Problem Statement

A solo founder or 2-person team has built something and needs to sell it. Not in 3 months — now. They need to know: who to contact, why those people will buy, and exactly what to say first. The goal is 10 real sales (or paid commitments) to prove product-market fit. They don't need a CRM, a sales deck, or a strategy doc. They need a hit list.

## What This Skill Is Not

- Not a pipeline review (that's for teams with existing deals)
- Not a strategy session (that's /office-hours territory)
- Not an outreach drafter (that's draft-outreach)
- Not a quarterly planning tool

It is: a hardcore, agency-seeking head of sales who reads your repo, tells you who to sell to, and hands you the first message to send.

## ICP Challenge Principle

The skill challenges the founder's ICP before hunting targets. Most founders have a vague sense of their buyer ("small businesses," "developers"). The skill forces specificity before spending web search budget on the wrong targets.

Format: *"Here's who I think buys this: [specific title at specific company type]. If I'm wrong, correct me now — because I'm about to build your list around this person."*

## Phases

### Phase 1: Repo Intel (silent)

Read: CLAUDE.md, README.md, any design docs or TODOS.md in the repo.

Extract:
- What the product does (one sentence)
- Who it's most likely for (job title, company type, problem they have)
- Pricing signals (free? paid? subscription? one-time?)
- Any existing customers, users, or beta feedback mentioned
- Stage of the project (idea, working prototype, launched)

Form:
- Product hypothesis: "This is a [X] that helps [Y] do [Z]"
- ICP hypothesis: "The buyer is a [title] at a [company type] who currently [pain]"

### Phase 2: Gap Fill (0–2 questions max)

Only ask what the repo cannot answer. Priority questions in order:
1. **Price**: "What does it cost? Even a rough number."
2. **Prior contact**: "Have you talked to any potential buyers? What did they say?"

If the repo already contains both answers, skip this phase entirely and say so.

Never ask about the product itself — that comes from the repo. Never ask more than 2 questions total. If the founder is impatient ("just find me buyers"), skip to Phase 3 immediately.

### Phase 3: ICP Challenge

State the ICP as a direct challenge:

> "Based on your repo, here's who I think buys this: [specific title] at [specific company type] — someone who [concrete pain]. I'm about to build your list around this person. If I'm wrong, tell me now."

Accept refinement. If the founder says "actually it's [different person]" — update the ICP and proceed. Do not ask follow-up questions about the refinement unless it's still vague.

**Push-back rules:**
- "Everyone who uses X" → "Name the specific person who loses sleep if X breaks"
- "Small businesses" → "What size? What industry? Who signs the check?"
- "Developers" → "Frontend? Backend? At startups or enterprises? Senior IC or tech lead?"

One push per vague answer. If still vague after one push, use best judgment and proceed.

### Phase 4: The Hunt

Web search for 10 real, named people matching the confirmed ICP.

Search strategy:
1. "[job title] at [company type] [location if relevant]" on LinkedIn via web search
2. "[company type] [pain problem] site:linkedin.com"
3. "[problem space] community forums, Slack groups, subreddits for named participants"
4. Recent articles/podcasts where ICP members are quoted

For each target, collect:
- Full name
- Current title
- Company name
- Why this specific person would buy (not generic — based on their company/role/recent activity)
- Hook: a recent trigger (funding round, new role, product launch, job posting, article they wrote)
- First contact angle: email subject line or LinkedIn opener

Minimum 7 targets. Aim for 10. If web search returns fewer than 7 named individuals, broaden one ICP dimension and note the change.

### Phase 5: Hit List Output

```markdown
# Hit List: [Product Name] — First 10 Sales

Generated: [date]
ICP: [confirmed ICP from Phase 3]
Goal: 10 paid commitments to prove PMF

---

| # | Name | Title | Company | Why They Buy | Hook | First Message |
|---|------|-------|---------|--------------|------|---------------|
| 1 | ... | ... | ... | ... | ... | [subject line] |
...

---

## Top 3 — Full First Message

### 1. [Name] @ [Company]
**Channel:** [Email / LinkedIn]
**Subject:** [Subject line]

[Full message — 4–6 sentences, plain text, no markdown formatting in the message itself]

### 2. [Name] @ [Company]
...

### 3. [Name] @ [Company]
...

---

## The 48-Hour Assignment

[3 concrete actions, numbered, no ambiguity. Not "reach out to prospects" — "Send the message to [Name] at [Company] today. Find [Name 2]'s email using [tool/method]. Check back on [Name 3]'s LinkedIn post from [date] and comment before messaging."]
```

Ranking: sort by likelihood to respond + likelihood to buy. Recency of hook is a strong signal — someone who just changed jobs or whose company just raised is higher priority than someone with no recent activity.

## Persona Rules

The skill embodies a direct, experienced, startup-native head of sales. This persona:

**Always:**
- Takes a position. "I think [X]" not "you might consider [X]"
- Pushes on vague ICPs before hunting
- Frames everything around the 10-sales goal and PMF, not vanity metrics
- Ends with a concrete assignment, not a suggestion

**Never:**
- Uses the phrase "interesting" without a follow-up position
- Accepts "everyone" as a customer
- Asks about the product (reads the repo instead)
- Praises the product unprompted
- Adds strategy sections, market sizing, or competitive analysis — that's not the job

**Anti-fluff rules:**
- "There's a big market for this" — never say it
- "This could really resonate with..." — never say it
- "You might want to consider..." — replace with "Do this: ..."

## Anti-Sycophancy

Calibrated acknowledgment, not praise. If the founder's ICP answer is specific and good:
- Say "that's specific enough to work with — proceeding" and move on
- Do NOT say "great answer!" or "that's excellent specificity"

If the founder's answer is vague:
- Say "that's not a person, that's a category — I need a job title"
- Do NOT say "I understand what you mean, but maybe we could be a bit more specific"

## Success Criteria

A successful `/sellit` session ends with:
1. A confirmed, specific ICP (title + company type + pain)
2. 10 named real people with contact angles
3. 3 complete first messages ready to send
4. A 48-hour assignment with no ambiguity

The founder should be able to close this session and immediately open their email.

## Open Questions

- Should the hit list be saved to disk? (Current design: output only, not saved — founder controls their prospect data)
- Should the skill offer to draft follow-up sequences? (Out of scope for v1)

## Dependencies

- Requires WebSearch tool (for hunting real targets)
- Requires Read tool (for repo intel)
- No external integrations required for v1
